## ‚ö†Ô∏è Things to Keep in Mind

### For Football Fans:
- **This is for fun!** Football is unpredictable - that's what makes it beautiful. No model can account for last-minute bangers, controversial VAR decisions, or the sheer chaos of derby days.
- **Form ‚â† Guaranteed Success**: Just because a team dominates the Champions League doesn't mean they'll automatically walk the Bundesliga. Different leagues, different vibes.
- **Injuries & Transfers Matter**: The model works with historical data. If your star striker gets injured or your best defender transfers mid-season, real-world results will vary.
- **Underdogs Exist**: Remember Leicester City? Yeah, machine learning didn't see that coming either. ü¶ä

### For Developers/Data Scientists:
- **Data Quality**: The predictions are only as good as the data fed into the model. Make sure you're using reliable, up-to-date sources.
- **Model Limitations**: This is a learning project. It's not a professional betting tool (please don't bet your life savings on this).
- **Bundesliga-Specific Factors**: The Bundesliga has unique characteristics (50+1 rule, winter break, etc.) that might not translate directly from other leagues.
- **Sample Size**: Football seasons are relatively short (34 games). Small sample sizes can lead to variance in predictions.
- **External Factors**: Managerial changes, team chemistry, fan support, and weather conditions aren't always captured in stats.
